Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. Such shifts can be natural, due to changes in the sun’s activity or large volcanic eruptions. Human activities, however, are the main drivers today, increasing greenhouse gases that contribute to climate change.

Causes and Effects of Climate Change

Fossil fuels – coal, oil, and gas – are the largest contributor to global climate change, accounting for over 75% of greenhouse gas emissions and nearly 90% of carbon dioxide emissions. These emissions trap the sun’s heat, leading to global warming and disrupting the balance of nature.

Causes of Climate Change

Fossil Fuel Power Plants

Burning coal, oil, and gas in energy, transportation, and industry increases greenhouse gases, trapping heat and raising Earth’s temperature.

Manufacturing Goods

From extraction of materials to production and transport, manufacturing releases greenhouse gases, often relying on fossil fuels.

Deforestation

Cutting forests releases stored carbon dioxide and reduces the planet’s ability to absorb emissions. Around 12 million hectares are destroyed annually.

Transportation

Most vehicles burn fossil fuels, making transport a major source of emissions. Road vehicles, planes, and ships significantly contribute to carbon dioxide levels.

Food Production

Agriculture contributes through land clearing, livestock digestion, fertilizer use, and energy consumption, making it a major source of methane and carbon dioxide.

Buildings

Residential and commercial buildings consume over half of electricity, much of it generated from fossil fuels, contributing to rising emissions.

Consumption

Household energy use, diet, waste, and product consumption all contribute to emissions. The wealthiest 1% account for more greenhouse gases than the poorest 50%.

Effects of Climate Change

Hotter Temperatures

Each decade since the 1980s has been warmer than the last, increasing risks of heat-related illness, wildfires, and Arctic warming.

More Severe Storms

Warmer oceans intensify storms, causing flooding, destruction, and economic losses.

Increased Drought

Water scarcity, crop failures, and ecosystem stress are becoming more frequent due to climate change.

Rising Seas

Oceans absorb most of the excess heat, expand, and rise due to melting ice, threatening coastal communities.

Loss of Species

One million species are at risk of extinction, accelerated by extreme weather, pests, and habitat loss.

Food Security

Extreme weather, ocean acidification, and loss of arable land are undermining global food systems.

Health Risks

Climate change worsens air pollution, disease spread, malnutrition, and disaster-related health crises.

Poverty and Displacement

Millions are displaced annually by climate-related disasters, deepening poverty and vulnerability.

Statistics

Humans are responsible for virtually all global heating over the last 200 years. Earth’s surface is now about 1.2°C warmer than in the late 1800s, with the last decade being the hottest on record. Consequences include droughts, floods, fires, rising seas, and biodiversity loss.

Climate News in the Philippines

The Philippines has faced increasingly severe typhoons, such as Super Typhoon Odette (2021) and multiple storms in 2024. Infrastructure projects meant to mitigate flooding have often been plagued by corruption and inefficiency.

Advocacy Groups in the Philippines

The Philippine Movement for Climate Justice (PMCJ)

A coalition pushing for climate justice, systemic reforms, and social transformation in the Philippines.

Youth Advocate for Climate Action Philippines (YACAP)

A youth-led alliance advocating climate justice, modeled after Fridays for Future, with chapters nationwide.

Public Opinion

Surveys show 97.6% of Filipinos view climate change as a serious threat, the highest concern rate in Southeast Asia, reflecting urgent public sentiment.

Trends and Innovations

The current trend of innovation in the Philippines involves the shift from fossil fuel based energy generation and usage to instead use clean and renewable energy, building of flood control infrastructure, and reforestation attempts.

Modernization of Transportation

Various parts in the Philippines are now adapting electricity-powered vehicles such as E-Jeepneys to provide a more energy efficient alternative to traditional gasoline or diesel powered jeepneys.

Building of Flood Control Infrastructure

Flood mitigation or control projects were iniated by the Philippine government to help the communities that suffer from severe floodings.

Reforestation Efforts

Climate Change groups in the Philippines perform reforestation efforts to protect its rich biodiversity, enhance the climate resilience and prevent soil erosion and natural disasters like landslides and flash floods.
